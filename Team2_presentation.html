01 · Problem Statement

The Real Pain of Upper-Limb Amputees

Neural Pathways Degenerate Motor cortex areas begin to remap and shrink after amputation. Without early stimulation the brain "forgets" how to drive the hand — every week without practice is lost neural real estate.
No Way to Test Before Buying No way to test patient–device compatibility before fabrication. Mismatches found after delivery at $20k–$100k per device. 30–50 % abandon within year 1.
Poor EMG Control Hand has 20+ DOF but controllers distinguish only 2–6 modes. Pattern-recognition accuracy degrades outside the lab — the controller is the bottleneck.

Root Cause: Amputees can't see or practice with a prosthetic hand before they get one — and every day without motor practice, the neural pathways that would control it are fading.

The reality of prosthetic hand control today

01 · Problem Statement — Clinical & Industry

Three Walls the Field Hits

Measurement Wall

Socket fitting relies on 2–3 in-person visits with caliper measurements. The residual limb changes volume daily. Current process can't track that.

Motor Relearning Wall

Patients must consciously produce 49 labelled movements following verbal instructions they don't understand. Primary source of calibration failure.

Controller Wall

EMG classifiers are calibrated once and frozen. Signals drift across sessions. Performance degrades. No adaptation between visits.
