01 · Problem Statement

The Three Walls Every Amputee Hits

Upper-limb amputees face a sequential series of failures — each one caused by the absence of digital intelligence at a step where it could have intervened.

03 · Layer 0

Smartphone Photogrammetry
Replacing the $10,000 Scanner

L0
Structure-from-Motion 3D Reconstruction
COLMAP · Open3D · OpenCV ArUco · 30-second video input

How It Works

The patient records a 30-second video circling their residual limb with their smartphone. Three printed ArUco markers placed near the limb give the algorithm absolute scale — the AI knows their physical size (e.g., 5 cm × 5 cm) and uses this to anchor all distance measurements.

Governing Theory: Structure-from-Motion (SfM)

SfM solves the inverse problem: given multiple 2D images of a scene from different viewpoints, recover the 3D structure of the scene and the camera poses simultaneously.

Projection Model
$$\mathbf{x}_i = \mathbf{K} \left[ \mathbf{R}_i \mid \mathbf{t}_i \right] \mathbf{X}$$

where $\mathbf{x}_i$ is the 2D image point, $\mathbf{K}$ is the camera intrinsic matrix, $[\mathbf{R}_i \mid \mathbf{t}_i]$ is the extrinsic pose of frame $i$, and $\mathbf{X}$ is the 3D world point.

Bundle Adjustment (Joint Optimisation)
$$\min_{\{\mathbf{R}_i, \mathbf{t}_i\}, \{\mathbf{X}_j\}} \sum_{i,j} \left\| \mathbf{x}_{ij} - \pi(\mathbf{R}_i, \mathbf{t}_i, \mathbf{X}_j) \right\|^2$$

Jointly minimises 2D reprojection error across all frames and all 3D points. COLMAP solves this with Levenberg–Marquardt.

ArUco Scale Recovery
$$s = \frac{L_{\text{physical}}}{L_{\text{reconstructed}}} \qquad \mathbf{X}_{\text{calibrated}} = s \cdot \mathbf{X}_{\text{SfM}}$$

A known physical length $L_{\text{physical}}$ on the printed marker calibrates the metric scale of the entire reconstruction.

04 · Layer 1

Socket Pressure Simulation
Replacing Physical Test Sockets

L1
MuJoCo Soft-Body Contact Mechanics
Rigid socket · compliant tissue model · daily volume fluctuation

Physics Being Modelled

The residual limb is a soft viscoelastic body. The socket is rigid. When the patient wears the socket, the contact forces between the two surfaces determine comfort and pressure ulcer risk. MuJoCo's contact solver models this entirely in software.

MuJoCo Contact Compliance Model (Impedance)
$$f_n = \frac{1}{d_1}\left(\frac{d}{d_2}\right)^3 + \frac{1}{d_1} \quad \text{where} \quad \mathbf{solref} = [d_t, d_r], \quad \mathbf{solimp} = [d_1, d_2, d_w]$$

MuJoCo parameterises contact stiffness via $(d_t, d_r)$ — the reference time constant and damping ratio. Skin tissue is modelled with $d_t = 0.02$ (20 ms response), $d_r = 1.0$. The socket is nearly rigid: $d_t = 0.001$.

Pressure at Contact Point $k$
$$P_k = \frac{F_{n,k}}{A_k} \quad [\text{kPa}]$$

$F_{n,k}$ is the normal contact force at point $k$, $A_k$ is the associated contact area. Clinically dangerous threshold: $P_k > 40$ kPa sustained → ulcer risk.

Daily Volume Change Model
$$V(t) = V_0 \cdot \left(1 - \alpha \cdot \ln\left(1 + \frac{t}{T_{1/2}}\right)\right)$$

Empirical model from Lilja & Öberg (1997). $V_0$ = morning volume, $\alpha \approx 0.05$ (5% total shrinkage), $T_{1/2} \approx 4$ hours. Used to simulate how fit changes from morning to evening in the same socket.

Clinical Output: Pressure Heatmap

The simulator produces a 2D unwrapped pressure map of the limb surface under four activity conditions (rest, walking, lifting 1 kg, lifting 5 kg). Each activity applies different axial and shear loading profiles to the socket. The clinician sees a colour-coded map before the socket is manufactured.

⚠️ Volume change simulation: The same socket design is evaluated at $t = 0$ hr (morning), $t = 4$ hr (midday), and $t = 8$ hr (evening) using the volume decay model. If fit quality drops below threshold at any time point, the design is flagged for a liner recommendation before fabrication.
05 · Layer 2

Contralateral Mirror Bootstrap
Zero-Training EMG Labelling

L2
MediaPipe Hands + Automatic Dataset Generation
Intact hand → auto-label residual sEMG · 10-minute calibration → 3 minutes

The Insight

Traditional EMG calibration requires the patient to consciously produce 49 labelled movements following verbal instructions they do not yet understand. This is the primary source of calibration failure and patient frustration. The contralateral method removes this entirely: the patient just uses their intact hand naturally while the webcam watches it. The intact hand acts as a perfect label source for the residual limb's sEMG.

Governing Theory: Cross-Limb Transfer

Bilateral motor tasks activate the same motor cortex regions symmetrically. When a person attempts to close their intact hand, their residual forearm muscles produce an sEMG pattern that is structurally analogous — shifted in amplitude and channel allocation, but sharing the same temporal structure. The Ninapro Foundation Model understands this mapping.

MediaPipe Hand Landmark → Joint Angle
$$\theta_{ij} = \arccos\left(\frac{\mathbf{v}_{i} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{j}}{\|\mathbf{v}_{i}\|\|\mathbf{v}_{j}\|}\right)$$

$\mathbf{v}_i$ and $\mathbf{v}_j$ are vectors along adjacent phalanges derived from MediaPipe's 21 hand landmarks. Applied across all finger joints → 21-DOF pose $\hat{\mathbf{q}}$.

Automatic EMG Labelling
$$y_t = \underset{c \in \mathcal{C}}{\arg\max} \; \cos\text{-sim}\!\left(\hat{\mathbf{q}}_t,\; \mathbf{q}^*_c\right)$$

At time $t$, the webcam delivers pose $\hat{\mathbf{q}}_t$. The label is the Ninapro movement class $c$ whose canonical pose $\mathbf{q}^*_c$ is most similar. This label is assigned to the concurrent sEMG window without any patient input.

What the Patient Sees On Screen

The digital twin hand on the monitor mirrors the intact hand in real time — exactly as MediaPipe sees it. The patient experiences this as watching their intact hand control a prosthetic hand on screen. After 10 minutes of natural movement, the system has collected a fully labelled personal sEMG calibration dataset. The patient experienced it as playing with a hand mirror.

✓ Connection to Ninapro: The canonical poses $\mathbf{q}^*_c$ are derived from the DB8 and DB9 kinematic recordings — the average joint configuration for each of the 49 Ninapro movement classes, computed across all intact subjects in the database.
06 · Layer 3

The Biological Twin
Foundation World Model for sEMG

L3
Temporal CNN-LSTM · Pre-trained on Ninapro DB1–DB10
sEMG → 21-DOF intended hand kinematics · EWC continual learning

Architecture

The network takes a 200 ms sliding window of 12-channel sEMG at 2 kHz as input and outputs the 21-DOF hand joint angle vector the patient intended to produce. It is not a classifier — it outputs continuous kinematics, which makes the digital twin move fluidly rather than snapping between discrete states. 

# Biological Twin: Temporal CNN + LSTM → 21-DOF kinematic decoder
class BiologicalTwin(nn.Module):
    def __init__(self):
        super().__init__()
        # Stage 1: Temporal CNN — learns local muscle activation patterns
        self.cnn = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Conv1d(12, 64,  kernel_size=5, padding=2),  # 12 EMG channels
            nn.BatchNorm1d(64),  nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv1d(64, 128, kernel_size=3, padding=1),
            nn.BatchNorm1d(128), nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Conv1d(128, 256, kernel_size=3, padding=1),
            nn.AdaptiveAvgPool1d(32)   # → (batch, 256, 32)
        )
        # Stage 2: LSTM — captures inter-movement sequential context
        self.lstm = nn.LSTM(256, 256, num_layers=2,
                             batch_first=True, dropout=0.3)
        # Stage 3: Kinematic decoder → 21 joint angles
        self.decoder = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(256, 128), nn.ReLU(),
            nn.Linear(128, 21)     # 21 DOF: 5 fingers × 3 joints + thumb
        )

    def forward(self, emg):               # emg: (B, 12, 400) — 200ms at 2kHz
        x = self.cnn(emg)               # (B, 256, 32)
        x, _ = self.lstm(x.permute(0,2,1))  # (B, 32, 256)
        return self.decoder(x[:, -1, :])  # last timestep → joint angles (°)

Governing Theory: Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC)

Naive fine-tuning causes catastrophic forgetting — the model forgets what it learned in earlier sessions as it adapts to the most recent one. EWC prevents this by penalising changes to weights that were important in previous sessions.

EWC Loss Function
$$\mathcal{L}_\text{EWC}(\theta) = \mathcal{L}_\text{task}(\theta) + \frac{\lambda}{2} \sum_i F_i \left(\theta_i - \theta^*_i\right)^2$$

$\theta^*_i$ are the parameter values after the previous session. $F_i$ is the diagonal of the Fisher information matrix — how important was weight $i$ to the previous task. $\lambda$ controls the strength of the memory constraint. High $F_i$ weights are penalised heavily for changing.

Fisher Information Diagonal
$$F_i = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x} \sim \mathcal{D}}\!\left[ \left(\frac{\partial \log p(y \mid \mathbf{x}; \theta)}{\partial \theta_i}\right)^2 \right]$$

Computed empirically over the previous session's clean examples $\mathcal{D}$.

07 · Layer 4

The Digital Twin Hand
Physics-Based Motor Feedback

L4
MuJoCo 21-DOF Prosthetic Hand Model
Rigid-body kinematics · contact solver · tendon coupling · task objects

What the Digital Twin Models

🦴 Rigid Body Kinematics

5 fingers × 3 hinge joints = 15 DOF. Each joint has anatomically correct range-of-motion limits, damping (models muscle tone), and stiffness (models tendon elasticity). Joint coupling via MuJoCo tendons mirrors how real fingers are physically coupled.

🤝 Contact & Grasp Stability

MuJoCo's contact solver computes normal forces, friction cone conditions, and penetration depth at every finger-object contact point. A grasp is "stable" only when ≥3 contact points satisfy the friction cone — same as real mechanics.

📦 Task Objects

Physics-accurate objects of varying mass, friction, and shape: foam ball (8cm, 50g), water cup (cylinder, 200g), coin (disc, 5g), door key (irregular), elastic band. Each stresses different aspects of control.

⚡ Fatigue Endurance Model

Maximum hold duration estimated from the patient's session history and updated per session. When predicted hold capacity is exceeded, the digital twin shows grip loosening — training the patient to pace their contractions.

Governing Theory: Grasp Stability (Friction Cone)

Friction Cone Constraint at Contact Point $k$
$$\sqrt{f_{t,k}^2 + f_{b,k}^2} \leq \mu \cdot f_{n,k}$$

$f_{n,k}$: normal force, $f_{t,k}, f_{b,k}$: tangential (friction) forces, $\mu$: friction coefficient of the object surface. Satisfaction at all contact points ⟹ no-slip grasp = task success.

Reward Function (extends existing env.py)
$$r_t = \underbrace{2.0 \cdot (-d_t)}_{\text{distance}} + \underbrace{1.2 \cdot (-\|\Delta a_t\|^2)}_{\text{smoothness}} + \underbrace{0.01 \cdot (-\|a_t\|^2)}_{\text{effort}} + \underbrace{3.0 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{stable grasp}]}_{\text{task bonus}}$$

$d_t$: fingertip-to-object distance. $\Delta a_t = a_t - a_{t-1}$: action jerk. The existing env.py already implements the first three terms. The stable grasp bonus is added for the hand model.

08 · Layer 5

The RL Virtual Therapist
Co-Adaptation and Curriculum Learning

L5
Multi-Agent PPO System · Overnight Co-Adaptation Loop
Virtual Patient Agent vs. AI Controller Agent · Curriculum policy personalisation

Two Distinct RL Problems

A — The RL Therapist: Curriculum Policy

An RL agent that observes every session and decides what to prescribe next. It learns a curriculum policy that maximises the patient's learning rate while keeping them safely between boredom and frustration.

MDP Formulation — RL Therapist
$$\mathcal{S} = \left[\bar{a}_{1:K},\; s_n,\; f_t,\; \Delta t_\text{surg}\right] \quad \mathcal{A} = \left[\text{task}, \text{difficulty}, \text{duration}\right] \quad r = \dot{\bar{a}}_k - \beta_1 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\bar{a}_k < 0.4] - \beta_2 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\bar{a}_k > 0.95]$$

$\bar{a}_{1:K}$: mean accuracy per movement class over last 3 sessions. $s_n$: session number. $f_t$: current fatigue level. $\Delta t_\text{surg}$: days since surgery. $\dot{\bar{a}}_k$: accuracy improvement rate (learning signal). $\beta_1$: frustration penalty (accuracy below 40%). $\beta_2$: boredom penalty (accuracy above 95% for 3 sessions).

B — The Co-Adaptation Loop: Virtual Patient vs. AI Controller

While the patient sleeps, two agents run against each other in the MuJoCo sandbox. This is the mechanism that makes the controller improve without requiring the patient to do anything.

── OVERNIGHT CLOUD LOOP (4 hours, ~100,000 episodes) ──
Agent 1 : Virtual Patient Model generates synthetic sEMG(intent, fatigue_level)
↓ noisy, patient-specific signal drawn from fitted Gaussian
Agent 2 : PPO Controller Agent decodes signal → commands digital twin → attempts task
↓ MuJoCo physics judges: stable grasp? task complete?
Reward task_success + decode_confidence + smoothness
↓ PPO updates Controller Agent weights
Agent 1 slightly drifts its distribution (simulates next-day variability)
↓ loop repeats until 4-hour budget exhausted
Result : Improved weights pushed to patient app before morning session
Virtual Patient Signal Generation
$$\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_t \sim \mathcal{N}\!\left(\boldsymbol{\mu}_c,\; \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_c\right) \cdot (1 - \alpha_f \cdot f_t)$$

$c$: intended movement class. $\boldsymbol{\mu}_c, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_c$: mean and covariance of this patient's sEMG for class $c$, estimated from all recorded sessions. $f_t \in [0,1]$: fatigue level. $\alpha_f$: fatigue-induced amplitude decay coefficient fit from DB6 within-session amplitude curves.

PPO Surrogate Objective (Policy Update)
$$\mathcal{L}^\text{PPO}(\theta) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\!\left[ \min\!\left(r_t(\theta)\,\hat{A}_t,\; \text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1-\varepsilon, 1+\varepsilon)\,\hat{A}_t\right) \right]$$

$r_t(\theta) = \pi_\theta(a_t|s_t) / \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(a_t|s_t)$: probability ratio. $\hat{A}_t$: generalised advantage estimate. $\varepsilon = 0.2$ (standard). This is the same PPO used in the existing train.py, extended to the hand model.

10 · Evaluation

How Every Claim Is Measured

Task Success: Three-Layer Evaluator

Every patient attempt is simultaneously scored at three levels. The composite score is the primary metric consumed by the RL Therapist's reward function.

Composite Attempt Score
$$S_\text{attempt} = 0.25 \cdot S_\text{signal} + 0.35 \cdot S_\text{kinematic} + 0.40 \cdot S_\text{physics}$$
Layer 1 — Signal Decoding Score
$$S_\text{signal} = p_\text{max} \cdot \mathbf{1}[\hat{c} = c^*]$$

$p_\text{max}$: CNN softmax confidence. $\hat{c}$: predicted class. $c^*$: intended class. Zero if wrong class is decoded regardless of confidence.

Layer 2 — Kinematic Accuracy Score
$$S_\text{kinematic} = \max\!\left(0,\; 1 - \frac{\bar{\delta}_\theta}{45°}\right) \cdot \min\!\left(\frac{T_\text{hold}}{2\text{s}},\; 1\right)$$

$\bar{\delta}_\theta$: mean joint angle error across 21 DOF vs. target pose. $T_\text{hold}$: seconds the patient sustained the correct pose (normalised to 2 seconds).

Layer 3 — Physics Task Score
$$S_\text{physics} = 0.3 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{contact}] + 0.3 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{stable}] + 0.4 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{complete}]$$

contact: ≥3 finger-object contact points active. stable: friction cone satisfied at all contacts. complete: object reached target zone.
